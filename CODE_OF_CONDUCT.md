# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in the OpenPAKT project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We commit to fostering an open, welcoming, and respectful community.

---

## Expected Behaviour

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the project and community
- Showing empathy towards other contributors

---

## Unacceptable Behaviour

Examples of unacceptable behaviour include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or abusive language
- Personal attacks or insults
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or inflammatory comments
- Publishing private information without permission
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional environment

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing the standards of acceptable behaviour.

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

---

## Reporting Issues

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project maintainers via GitHub issues or direct
communication at mailto:admin@meisterware.com

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

---

## Enforcement Actions

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate in response to violations of this Code of Conduct.

Possible consequences include:

- Warning the contributor
- Temporary suspension from project participation
- Removal of contributions
- Permanent ban from the project

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues
- pull requests
- discussions
- project documentation
- community interactions related to the project
